What is a lead?

A lead is not yet a customer, but can be any person, organization, or company that may be interested in working with Illumina, purchasing our products, or using our services.

Leads can be manually entered, automatically entered via our Marketing management tool (Pardot), or imported via lists into campaigns.

When you are ready to import a list of leads into SFDC, check out the Prospecting Process and Tools tile to learn how to import lists and create and manage campaigns.

What's the difference between an MQL and AQL?

Marketing Qualified (MQL) status occurs when an individual requests to be contacted by an Illumina Sales Rep.

Examples of how customers can request to be contacted:

1. Online Forms: “Contact Me” or “Request a Quote”

2. In Person: Lead requests sales follow-up at a seminar or tradeshow

Automation Qualified (AQL) status occurs when an individual hits a certain score based on an algorithm that looks at the individual's activity.

Example activities that impact scoring include:

• Registration for an event (Seminar, webinar, tradeshow event)

• Attendance at an event (Seminar, webinar, tradeshow event)

• Email Opens and Clicks

• Website content views

How are tiers determined?

All newly created accounts are automatically grouped as Tier 3.

If a new account is added to a customer hierarchy with Tier 1 or 2, SalesOps will request the update of its tier so all accounts in the hierarchy are aligned.

Every month, SalesOps work with Inside Sales to review the accounts created in the previous month and request tiering updates if required.

Customers are re-tiered in an annual process through discussion between Inside Sales Managers & DSMs. Accounts are typically moved to Tier 2 when they meet a certain consumables threshold or have instrument opportunities that would be better covered by the TAMs.

How do I submit a new account for ERP number generation?

The ERP Customer ID is a customer’s unique identifier that is assigned once the address on the account has been validated and submitted for ERP assignment by the Data Stewards.

1. To submit the account for an ERP Customer ID, click Submit Prospect. The status of the account must be ‘Unverified’.

2. It will take you to the below screen. If the Market segmentation hasn’t already been filled in, this will need to be done first

Fill the required market segmentation in, and press save at the bottom of the screen.

Once the segmentation is filled in, you can then select the provided address and press ‘Submit to ERP’

3. This will then submit the prospect to the Data Stewards Master Data Governance (MDG) queue where they can process the request and assign the ERP Customer ID. The account status will now change to ‘prospect validation’

4. Once the Data Stewards have approved the prospect validation, the account status will change to ‘Active Prospect’ and the ERP Customer ID will be assigned. You will also receive an auto-generated email from SFDC with ERP Customer ID.

No quoting can be completed on the account until an ERP Customer ID is assigned, but the opportunity can be raised without an ERP Customer ID.

What are the definitions for the different account statuses I see on an account record?

- Unverified: Prospect account has not been submitted for validation.

- Prospect Validation: Prospect Account has been submitted for validation and is pending Data Steward approval. Once approved, it will be    allocated an ERP Id.

- Active: Account is ready for use.

- Rejected: Prospect Account has failed validation. See Account Notes section for the failure reason or contact the Data Steward team.

- Suspended: Account block is in place and cannot be used. Contact AR or Data Steward Teams for more information.

- Cancelled / Pending Obsolete: Account has been marked for deletion.

- Obsolete: Account cannot be used.

What are the different record types for opportunities?

An opportunity is a potential sale of Illumina products/services to a customer. Based on the sale channel, an opp can be:

• Direct – When Illumina sells directly to a customer and end user of the Illumina product/service

• Indirect – When Illumina sells an Illumina product/service through a channel partner.

Opportunities in SFDC can be broadly classified into 3 record types based on a combination of product and business scenarios:

1. Standard – High-Touch sales opportunities (Instruments, Software, Consumables, Service Contracts, Bundles, Tenders, Rentals, etc.)

2. Reorder & Custom Product – Reorder/Renewal and Custom Product opportunities - No Up/Cross Sell (Quote-to-Order only)-(Consumable Reorders, Service Contract Renewals, Software Renewals, Custom Product (First Buy)).

3. Field Services – Billable and Professional Services sales opportunities (Quote-to-Order only) (Break/fix, PM, Relocation, IQ/OQ, Consulting, Compliance, etc.)

When is an opportunity considered to be in a "Commit" status?

The key drivers that are used to calculate forecast are:

• Stage: The phase of the sales cycle in which the opportunity is at the time of the forecast (For example, Create, Develop etc.)

• Probability %: User-defined level of confidence in the opportunity successfully closing at the indicated value and on the estimated Close Date

All opportunity (including Stage and Probability) information flows from Salesforce to IBM Planning Analytics (TM1). Forecast categorization, refinement, & finalization is done in IBM Planning Analytics (TM1).

‍

The IBM Planning Analytics (TM1) forecast categories are populated as follows, based on probability value:

60-69% = Upside Category

70-79% = At Risk Category

80%+ = Forecasted Category

As long as an opportunity is still open, Key exit criteria can be updated on the opportunity record by clicking directly on a field.

What measures are included in my Rollup?

The territory rollup is the total amount committed to ship for the period based on what has already shipped, what Supply Chain is committing to ship, and what is committed to close and ship via SFDC Opportunities, and Calculated Turns.

‍

The following measures contribute to your territory rollup:

Shipments: The amount that has shipped and invoiced (if invoice only) in the period. Actual Goods Issue Date = Date of Shipment

‍

Backlog Due: The amount previously ordered and has been communicated to the customer as scheduled to ship during the period. Ordered and scheduled but not shipped or invoiced. Planned Goods Issue Date = Planned Shipment Date

‍

Commit Opportunities: SFDC Opportunities forecasted to close and ship in the period. (Opportunity must have a Probability of over 70%)

     • PO Received: SFDC Opportunities where customer PO has been received and current period shipment is expected, but the order is yet to process in SAP. It is common for items to remain in this status for 1-7 days. This status can be due to the order being in one of the following statuses: include contract review, new customer set-up.

     • Forecasted: SFDC Opportunities forecasted to close and ship in the period. SFDC Forecast Category is Forecasted and PO in Contracts flag is not checked, PO Number is blank and PO Received Date is blank. (Opportunities with a Probability of 80% or higher)

     • Forecasted At Risk: SFDC Opportunities with more risk of closing and shipping in the period, but that are still considered as committed. SFDC Forecast Category is Forecasted at Risk. (Opportunities with a Probability of 70% to 79%)

‍

Calc Turns (not forecasted by TAM): Calculated estimate of Consumables to be ordered and shipped during the remainder of the period based on the amount ordered and shipped from the same point in the prior quarter. Intent is to have an estimate run-rate of consumable sales as a starting point, rather than input named opportunities. All orders from prior quarter with 50K or less in consumables ordered and shipped within the end of the quarter are included in the run-rate.

Are Instrument Relocation Services covered by an active Illumina Warranty or Service Contract?

If relocation is done by third company or customer themselves, they will need an insurance. If relocation service provided by Illumina, we will cover for the risks.

‍

According to the relocation policy:

Base Warranty

If the instrument is under an Illumina base warranty during the relocation period and Illumina/Channel Partners engaged to provide the full relocation service, the instrument’s base warranty will automatically transfer to the new location. The base warranty will not cover any damage to the instrument that may be incurred during the relocation process. Additionally, a system health check is required to place the asset back under base warranty. Please note that relocation service is billable and not covered by base warranty.

Relocation of the instrument by buyer or by any third party not acting on behalf of or authorized by Illumina will result in termination of the base warranty.

Base warranty will only transfer if title remains with current customer. If title transfers the base warranty is no longer valid.

‍

Service Contracts

The service contract may transfer with the instrument to its new location if the instrument remains at the same account within the same country. If the instrument is relocated to any new location outside of its own account and country and had an active Illumina service contract, the previously active service contract will be cancelled on the earlier of the instrument decommission date or a date provided by the customer with a minimum of 30 days written notice. The service contract will not cover any damage to the instrument that may be incurred during the relocation process. If the service contract is canceled, a credit for the remaining pro-rated period of coverage will be provided. It is recommended that a new service contract is purchased for the new location.

What changes can I make to my quote?

1. If your quote is in “Approved” status, you must REVERT TO DRAFT to make any product, discounting, quantity, or expiration date changes.

  • If the quotation stage is in Approved, Generated, Presented, or Rejected, updating these fields on a quote changes the quote stage to Denied.

2. If your quote is currently in “In Review” status, you must RECALL the quote from the approval chain to make any product, discounting, quantity, or expiration date changes.

3. If your quote is in “Generated” or “Presented” status, the only changes you will be able to make are changes to the “Valid to Date” as long as it remains within the current quarter, updates to the MyIllumina email address, the ability to mark a quote as web visible, and the ability to mark a quote primary.

  • Any other changes will not be allowed and you will need to CLONE the quote to make your product, discounting, or quantity revisions.

What is a Tender?

Tendering is a formal process where businesses are invited to bid for contracts from public or private sector organisations, which need specific skills for a project, or goods and services on an ongoing basis. The tendering process is designed to be fair and transparent. When projects are publicly funded, or there are simply large sums at stake, organisations need to get the best value-for-money for tax payers or stakeholders, and demonstrate they have not awarded contracts unfairly.

What is an open tender?

An open tender allows anyone to submit a tender to supply the goods or services that are required. Generally, an advert will be placed giving notice that the contract is being tendered, offering an equal opportunity to any organisation to submit a tender.

What is an negotiated tender?

An negotiated tender is when a contracting authority approaches a single supplier based on their track-record or a previous relationship and the terms of the contract are then negotiated.

Negotiating with a single supplier may be appropriate for highly specialist contracts (where there may be a limited number of potential suppliers), or for extending the scope of an existing contract.

What is a VEAT?

A VEAT (Voluntary Ex-Ante Transparency Notice) can be published when a contracting authority has chosen a particular route to market. This route falls under the label of a closed competition tender.

It’s used when a contracting authority has awarded a contract to a supplier without first having published a tender notice (an open call for competition), which opens the work up to the rest of the market. They can then, voluntarily, choose to publish a VEAT to allow the market to challenge the decision.

What is a PQQ?

Pre-qualification questionnaire or ‘PQQ’ is a questionnaire completed by companies or providers that wish to be considered for a procurement activity or placed on an approved list. The purpose is to assess their general suitability in terms of financial and economic standing, technical capability and experience, quality assurance, health and safety procedures, environmental issues and equalities considerations.

What is a PIN?

Pre- Information Notice is a short notice indicating that a call for tenders is planned during the coming months. The publication of a PIN means that the deadline for submitting the tender from the publication of the call may be shortened.

How do I know what quote type to use for a tender?

When the tender has asked for a quote that is for a fixed number of products for a one-time purchase. The offer must remain valid for 3 months after submission date. e.g. Please supply quote for DNA sequencer and include 2 year warranty. This would be a standard quote.

When the tender has asked for a quote that is for a range of products. They have provided estimated annual usage figures only. e.g. Please supply quote for consumables to use with a NextSeq instrument, prices to be fixed for 12 months from the award date. This would be a standing quote.  

The tender team will be able to help establish which quote would be best for the tender and work with the quotes team to get the correct quote in time for the tender deadline.
