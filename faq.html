What is a lead?

A lead is not yet a customer, but can be any person, organization, or company that may be interested in working with Illumina, purchasing our products, or using our services.

Leads can be manually entered, automatically entered via our Marketing management tool (Pardot), or imported via lists into campaigns.

When you are ready to import a list of leads into SFDC, check out the Prospecting Process and Tools tile to learn how to import lists and create and manage campaigns.

What's the difference between an MQL and AQL?

Marketing Qualified (MQL) status occurs when an individual requests to be contacted by an Illumina Sales Rep.

Examples of how customers can request to be contacted:

1. Online Forms: “Contact Me” or “Request a Quote”

2. In Person: Lead requests sales follow-up at a seminar or tradeshow

Automation Qualified (AQL) status occurs when an individual hits a certain score based on an algorithm that looks at the individual's activity.

Example activities that impact scoring include:

• Registration for an event (Seminar, webinar, tradeshow event)

• Attendance at an event (Seminar, webinar, tradeshow event)

• Email Opens and Clicks

• Website content views

How do I convert a lead to a contact in SFDC?

To improve the deliverability of our customer communications, there will now be a tab on the lead object to validate someone’s email prior to converting them to a contact. The tool used for this will be Informatica and will ping the email address (on the backend but does not actually send email to recipient) to verify if email is valid and will track results and date of last verification.

To convert a lead to a contact, sales users must first verify the email address of a lead.

1.     Click on the Verify Email tab on the Lead record

2.     Click Verify

If the verification is successful:

3.     You’ll receive a Success message at the top ofthe screen

4.     “Email ID is verified” will appear on the leadrecord

5.     Email verification date will be applied

Why am I getting the below error when creating a new Lead?

The system will automatically search for duplicate records whenever a new Lead or Contact is created:

a. Refresh your screen 15-30 seconds after creating a new Lead record. If an existing duplicate Lead record was found, the system will automatically merge the two records and the record you created will no longer exist.

b. Refer to the system generated email to find the existing Lead record you should use

c. Leverage the global search bar (search bar at the top of your screen) to search for the existing Lead Record based on First and Last Name, Email or Phone Number.

What is the definition of an Active vs Inactive contact?

Contacts that are marked Is Active are records that are considered to be valid and are actively associated to an Illumina Account.

The Contact’s Is Active field can be found on the details section of a Contact Record:

Contacts that are deactivated (Is Active flag is not checked), cannot be used to create Opportunities, Quotations, Cases, or Work Orders.

If a user deactivates a Contact, they must enter a Deactivation Reason. A Deactivation Reason Comment is optional.

I’ve identified a duplicate contact. How do I submit a merge request?

To submit a merge request,

a. On the contact record, review any potential duplicate records

b. Once you have confirmed the contacts are true duplicates, click on the checkbox next to the record(s) you would like to merge

c. Click Submit Merge Request

d. Provide any relevant detail for the Data Steward team

e. Click Send Merge Request

You should receive an email confirmation summarising your request. Once the Data Steward team has processed your request, you will receive an email confirmation that the contact (s) have been merged.

Contact error on opportunity. What should I do?

The contact you are trying to use is currently not marked Is Active.

a. Search for a relevant active Contact and replace the Contact on the Opportunity.

b. If no Contact was found, reactivate the Contact by checking the Is Active box.

Once the Contact is reactivated, the Deactivation Reason and Deactivation Reason Comment will be erased.

If a user has accidentally removed the Is Active check, the box can always be rechecked, thus reactivating the contact.
